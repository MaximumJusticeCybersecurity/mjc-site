## 1. Purpose

Define the current public positioning, page hierarchy, calls to action, and cross-site requirements for Maximum Justice Cybersecurity and its CyberShield conversion path.

This document governs website implementation.  Durable trust doctrine belongs in Aegis.  CyberShield product behavior and demo content belong in the CyberShield repository.  Funnel and follow-up logic belong in MJC Revenue OS.

## 2. Current state finding

The current site metadata and `llms.txt` lead with:

   - Executive Operational Visibility.
   - TrustMap.
   - `Generate Your TrustMap`.

That language reflects an earlier CyberShield direction and is stale as the primary conversion path.

## 3. Current positioning hierarchy

### Firm

Maximum Justice Cybersecurity is the expert-led cybersecurity and AI governance firm.

### Founder

Dr. Max Justice is the founder, vCISO, Security SME, Cybersecurity SME, and human trust anchor.

### Current product and proof engine

CyberShield is the current AI Decision Assurance product and proof engine.

```text
AI-generated recommendation in -> defensible AI Trust Decision Record out
```

### Umbrella outcome

Executive Operational Visibility may remain a broader MJC outcome or category.  It shall not obscure the immediate buyer problem or override the current CyberShield wedge.

### Preserved or future capability

TrustMap may remain a secondary, preserved, or future capability.  It shall not be the primary CTA or imply that the current root experience is a TrustMap product.

## 4. Primary website objective

Move a qualified visitor toward one of two actions:

1. Challenge one AI-generated recommendation.
2. Schedule a qualified conversation about a controlled 3-to-5 recommendation pilot.

Primary CTA:

```text
Challenge One AI Recommendation
```

Secondary CTA:

```text
See the 3-Minute Vendor-Risk Example
```

Tertiary CTA:

```text
Discuss a Controlled Pilot
```

Do not present `Generate Your TrustMap` as the primary current CTA.

## 5. Homepage message sequence

The homepage shall follow this order:

1. **Buyer problem.** Organizations are using AI-generated recommendations in vendor, security, compliance, and executive decisions without a defensible review trail.
2. **Consequence.** Leadership may be unable to explain what was claimed, what evidence was missing, and why action was justified.
3. **Offer.** CyberShield turns one AI recommendation into an AI Trust Decision Record.
4. **Proof.** Show the vendor-risk example and its `Request Evidence` outcome.
5. **Control.** State that CyberShield does not approve the vendor and the organization retains authority.
6. **Credibility.** Present Dr. Max Justice's relevant experience concisely.
7. **Next action.** Challenge one recommendation or discuss a controlled pilot.

## 8. Who it is for

Initial public audiences:

   - CISOs and vCISOs.
   - Vendor-risk and third-party-risk teams.
   - GRC and compliance leaders.
   - Security reviewers.
   - AI governance leaders.
   - Executives accountable for high-impact AI-influenced recommendations.

Do not position the current offer as a general consumer product or finished enterprise SaaS platform.

## 9. Pilot presentation

Present the pilot as a controlled, expert-assisted engagement.

Minimum scope:

   - 3 to 5 AI-generated recommendations.
   - One decision domain.
   - Available supporting evidence.
   - One accountable decision owner.

Minimum deliverables:

   - AI Trust Decision Record for each recommendation.
   - Claims and evidence-gap matrix.
   - Risk If Wrong and confidence assessment.
   - Reviewer and escalation map.
   - Executive findings briefing.
   - Review-process improvement recommendations.

Until exact terms are approved, state:

```text
Pilot pricing and delivery schedule are confirmed after scope review.
```

Do not publish the proposed `$5,000`, `$7,500`, or `10 business day` terms without explicit owner approval.

## 11. Cross-site alignment

The MJC website shall link to the current preferred CyberShield public route, not internal QA or preserved legacy routes.

The CyberShield demo shall link back to:

   - MJC credibility and contact information.
   - The controlled pilot page.
   - The privacy or contact-use explanation when information is collected.

Funnels, landing pages, campaign pages, Calendly copy, email sequences, and social CTAs shall use the same product direction and CTA hierarchy.

## 12. SEO and machine-readable content

Update page metadata, structured data, `llms.txt`, social cards, titles, descriptions, and keyword language to reflect:

   - Maximum Justice Cybersecurity.
   - AI Decision Assurance.
   - AI Trust Decision Record.
   - Vendor-risk AI recommendation review.
   - Evidence gaps and human review.
   - vCISO and AI governance advisory.

Do not describe TrustMap as the current operational CyberShield engine unless the live experience and current governance support that claim.

## 13. Trust and claims boundaries

Public pages shall not claim:

   - Production readiness.
   - Autonomous approval.
   - Compliance certification.
   - Guaranteed risk reduction.
   - Complete model verification.
   - Live model-backed analysis when using static or synthetic demonstration logic.
   - Customer outcomes not supported by evidence.

Synthetic evidence shall be labeled.  Controlled demonstrations shall be distinguished from live customer reviews.
