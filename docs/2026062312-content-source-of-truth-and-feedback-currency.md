## Purpose

Prevent public website and funnel copy from drifting away from current MJC and CyberShield direction or being overwritten by delayed feedback formed against older builds.

## Authority by subject

1. Current explicit owner decision.
2. Current security, trusted-authority, human-agency, legal, and claims boundaries.
3. `content-governance.json`.
4. `docs/2026062312-mjc-site-positioning-and-conversion-requirements.md`.
5. Current CyberShield `governance-summary.json` and current dated content requirements.
6. Aegis trust-led customer-action and feedback-currency standards.
7. Approved page-specific implementation packet.
8. Current public code and metadata.
9. Historical website builds and delayed feedback.

When sources conflict, stop and record a supersession decision before changing public content.

## Delayed feedback rule

Delayed feedback may reveal a durable buyer problem.  It does not automatically revive the original CTA, page structure, category, or product scope.

Ask:

   - Does the buyer problem still exist?
   - Was the source reviewing the current Decision Assurance product or an older TrustMap experience?
   - Does the suggestion conflict with a later owner decision?
   - Can the insight improve the current journey without restoring obsolete scope?
   - Does the change require new pricing, proof, legal, security, or public claims approval?

## Cross-repository publishing gate

Before public release, verify:

   - CyberShield product direction and CTA match.
   - MJC firm positioning does not overstate CyberShield maturity.
   - Revenue OS funnels use the same CTA and qualification path.
   - Pricing and delivery terms are approved.
   - Route links point to current public routes.
   - Internal QA, build, capture, and fallback language is excluded.
   - Metrics and form submissions are verified before claiming success.

## Core rule

Public copy follows current recorded owner decisions, not whichever old document or delayed comment is easiest to find.
