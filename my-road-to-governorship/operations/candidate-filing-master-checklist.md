# Candidate Filing Master Checklist

## Candidate Identity and Eligibility

- [ ] Confirm legal name to appear on ballot
- [ ] Confirm residential address and municipality
- [ ] Confirm voter registration status and party enrollment
- [ ] Confirm office-specific residency duration
- [ ] Confirm age, citizenship, and other statutory qualifications
- [ ] Confirm no incompatible office or employment conflict
- [ ] Obtain written eligibility verification when appropriate

## Race Selection

- [ ] Confirm office appears on the applicable election ballot
- [ ] Confirm district, ward, region, or precinct
- [ ] Confirm term length and compensation
- [ ] Confirm current officeholder and whether incumbent is running
- [ ] Confirm party-primary, minor-party, political-body, or independent route
- [ ] Complete P-win assessment before filing
- [ ] Record formal go/no-go decision

## Ballot Access

- [ ] Obtain official nomination petition or nomination paper
- [ ] Verify circulation start date
- [ ] Verify filing deadline
- [ ] Verify required number of valid signatures
- [ ] Set internal signature target above legal minimum
- [ ] Train circulators on signer eligibility and affidavit rules
- [ ] Validate signatures before filing
- [ ] Notarize circulator affidavits when required
- [ ] File originals with correct election authority
- [ ] Obtain timestamped receipt
- [ ] Prepare for petition challenges

## Campaign Committee and Finance

- [ ] Determine whether a political committee is required
- [ ] Appoint treasurer and chair if required
- [ ] Open campaign bank account
- [ ] Obtain tax identification number if required
- [ ] File committee registration and candidate authorization forms
- [ ] Establish contribution and expenditure controls
- [ ] Create campaign-finance reporting calendar
- [ ] Preserve receipts, invoices, contribution records, and in-kind valuations
- [ ] Apply required disclaimers to communications

## Ethics and Disclosure

- [ ] File Statement of Financial Interests with each required authority
- [ ] Review conflict-of-interest rules
- [ ] Review government contractor and business-ownership implications
- [ ] Separate campaign, company, nonprofit, and personal resources
- [ ] Establish public-record retention protocol
- [ ] Review event use, food, gifts, and contribution treatment

## Campaign Operations

- [ ] Establish campaign email, phone, domain, and data system
- [ ] Establish volunteer confidentiality and conduct rules
- [ ] Establish voter-contact data handling rules
- [ ] Establish press and social approval process
- [ ] Establish opposition-research verification standard
- [ ] Establish incident-response and cybersecurity plan
- [ ] Build election-day legal, field, and communications plans

## Closeout

- [ ] File all post-election finance reports
- [ ] Pay or document campaign debts
- [ ] Preserve required records
- [ ] Close or retain committee according to law
- [ ] Complete after-action review
