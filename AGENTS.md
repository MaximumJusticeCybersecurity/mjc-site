## Human authority

Dr. Max Justice retains final authority over public positioning, pricing, guarantees, credentials, customer claims, legal or compliance claims, deployment, and public release.

Agents may analyze, draft, test, and prepare review branches and pull requests.  They may not silently publish, deploy, change domains, send communications, set pricing, create urgency or scarcity, or make unsupported proof claims.

## Feedback currency

Website copy shall not be changed merely because feedback arrived later.

For material feedback, record:

   - Original feedback date, when known.
   - Receipt date.
   - Page, build, or campaign reviewed.
   - Baseline commit.
   - Current-state comparison.
   - Accept, constrain, defer, reject, supersede, or request owner clarification.

Do not restore TrustMap-first, runtime-first, broad-platform, or obsolete CTA language from older materials without a current owner decision.

## Claims and trust rules

Public content shall:

   - Show proof before asking for unnecessary information.
   - Preserve meaningful customer choice and human authority.
   - Avoid fear inflation, fake urgency, artificial scarcity, or unsupported authority.
   - Distinguish current capability, controlled demonstration, advisory service, pilot, and roadmap.
   - Avoid production SaaS, certification, autonomous approval, or guaranteed-outcome claims.
   - Keep internal route, build, QA, capture, and source-of-truth details off public pages.
   - Use only approved credentials, metrics, customer names, logos, awards, and outcomes.

## Branch and release rule

Use task-specific branches.  Prepare a pull request.  Do not merge or deploy without explicit human approval.  Public release requires review of mobile, desktop, links, forms, analytics, metadata, accessibility, claims, and rollback.

## Pricing boundary

Exact CyberShield pilot pricing and delivery timing are not approved in this repository.  Use scope-review language until the owner approves exact public terms.
